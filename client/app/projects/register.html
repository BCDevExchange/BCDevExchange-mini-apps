What is a Project?

On the BCDevExchange, Projects are the containers for your work. They can be everything from unmet business needs that might be solved with technology to active development of digital resources, such as software code.

Projects might include digital resources, such as an application programing interfaces (APIs) or piece of code. Others might be in the Inspiration state – an idea that needs validation before moving forward. Projects can also include product roadmaps, personas or other documentation.

Why should your public sector organization list Projects with the BCDevExchange?

Many public sector organizations do similar things. As a result there is opportunity to collaborate on shared business needs. Entrepreneurs and developers can begin to help if they have a place to discover and understand your business needs.

Listing your Project on the BCDevExchange helps potential public and private sector collaborators find it and begin working with you.

It’s important that your Project has enough information for other public sector organizations, entrepreneurs and developers to see the opportunity to collaborate. For example, if your Project has a backlog of work, presenting the backlog on the BCDevExchange can help drive input on your Project direction, ideas and solutions — all geared toward a better result.

How do you register your Projects?


If you want some help with your Project, send us an email or create an Issue in our GitHub Projects Repo , and we’ll work with you to get it listed on the BCDevExchange.
